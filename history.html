HISTROY

Mukka Welfare Society was thriving for about 30 years as a center for Socio- Economic development. For inevitable reasons, the Society was wound up and the buildings became the property of Jyothi Mandir (Holy Spirit Church) Rev. Fr. Harold C. Dâ€™Souza, then parish priest converted these buildings into Holy Spirit English Medium School in order to impart quality and value based education to the students around Mukka. Dominican Sisters from Pakshikere rendered their services to this school until 2004, then Sisters of Notre Dame took over the responsibility of imparting education.

The school which was started with 8 students in the year 2001 has grown to 481, with 21 teaching and non-teaching staff and 4 part time teachers. It has excelled in quality both in Academics and in Extracurricular. For the past several years school has secured 100% results in SSLC Board Exams. According to the statistics of Education Department, Holy Spirit School gets 36th Rank out of 413 schools in the District.
